# HackerCard

This HackerCard is being created as part of the Hack Club HackerCard Jam. The project uses NFC (Near Field Communication) technology to provide a fast and convenient way to share my contact information with other people.

The HackerCard will be programmed so that when a smartphone or other NFC-enabled device is tapped against it, the device automatically opens a personalised online business card. This online page will contain key information about me, such as my name, email address, phone number, social media links, website, and any other details I choose to include.

A key feature of the online business card will be the ability for visitors to save my contact information directly to their phone's contacts list. Instead of manually typing details, users will be able to press a button to download or import a contact card (vCard), making the process much faster and reducing the chance of errors.

This system offers several advantages over traditional paper business cards. Information can be updated online without needing to reprint cards, links can be added to portfolios or projects, and contact details can be shared instantly. It is also more environmentally friendly because a single NFC card can be reused indefinitely.

The main goal of the project is to make networking and sharing contact information quicker and easier. For example, at events, clubs, conferences, or meetups, I can simply hand someone the card or allow them to tap it with their phone. Within a few seconds, they can view my details and save them directly to their contacts. This means I could share my information with around 20 people in just a few minutes, making the process significantly more efficient than exchanging details manually.

Overall, the HackerCard combines modern NFC technology with a digital business card to create a professional, convenient, and efficient way to connect with others and exchange contact information.
